Dynamic Lifeline (Template)

In recent years, Costa Rica's pristine tropical rainforests have been drastically destroyed due to deforestation, agriculture, cattle ranching, and accelerated urbanization, resulting in wildlife losing their habitats. And sloths, as arboreal animals, face more severe threats when looking for new habitats due to their slow movement and the need for a continuous canopy layer.

When crossing, sloths will traverse three major types of land: city, farmland, and fragmented rainforest. Based on different threats sloths may face, a variety of design strategies are also proposed to meet their specific needs when crossing three types of sites. Meanwhile, those strategies work cohesively with the existing environment while the human intervention levels gradually decrease. The project aims to create safe environments for sloths to migrate as well as help them return to nature.
